Eligibility

Students eligible for NWERC will be allowed to compete in the Nordic and national championships. All sites are allowed to have local contestants not participating in the championships. These will have a special tag in their team name, which the score-table spider will recognise.

Contest software architecture

Responsible: Open

Tightly coupled PC^2:
+ Very fair to contestants. Same start time, clarifications, and so on.
+ Smaller sites do not need to judge their own runs.
- Sites are not free to choose how to arrange their contest.
- Need wrapper scripts for compiling.
- Distributed judging gives problems with differences between UNIX and Windows environment for C/C++. (Not really a problem.)

PC^2 run locally, results merged by spider:
+ Very fault tolerant.
- You risk clarifications not reaching all sites if this is not handled carefully.

Heterogeneous setup: (One master site, some tightly coupled, some stand-alone/non-PC^2)
+ Sites are free to decide how to run their contest.
- Complex. Difficult to keep track of who does what and how.

The sites at which it is possible, will run tightly couled PC^2. A spider will collect results from sites which cannot or do not want to run tightly coupled.

ICPC tree

Responsible: Roy Anderson.

It is generally agreed that the Nordic events should get into the ICPC contest tree, as this gives several benefits. It is not clear how this is to be done. Two possible solutions are:

Norden is made an ICPC subregion. The national events are virtual.

Each Nordic country is made a subregion. The Nordic event is virtual.
+ We would probably get more slots for teams from NWERC in the world finals.
- Denmark, Finland and Iceland might not be big enough to become subregions. We are not even sure if there will be events there.

The NCPC was made a subregion of NWERC.

Results of tightly coupled test 2005-03-06

On Sunday March 6th we tested setting up PC2 in tightly coupled congiguration. We had 5 sites represented: Trondheim (Nils), Molde (Hans), Oslo (Erek), Umeå (Tomas) and Stockholm (Fredrik, Per, Mattias, Mikael).

Short version

Molde and Oslo could not connect due to NAT and a firewall respectively. Trondheim, Umeå and Stockholm went on to run the test and it worked well. See the full report for more details.

What's next

We knew that Firewalls and NAT would cause problems and also that these problems can be solved using a VPN. Or perhaps we can get around it if enough machines at each site are outside NAT and firewalls. At Lund I think the student machines are outside NAT and firewalls.

  • KTH and NTNU should approach the PC2-people in Shanghai and ask about their VPN-solution
  • Find out which sites are NAT:ed or behind firewalls, and if there are possibilities of getting "outside" NAT and firewalls there.
  • Run another test, preferably in April trying a VPN
